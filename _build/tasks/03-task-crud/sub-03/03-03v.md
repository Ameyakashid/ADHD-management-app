## SOUL.md Content Review

**Placement:** Task Management (line 68) sits between Boundaries (line 62) and State-Aware Adaptation (line 98). Logical flow: identity → rules → boundaries → task behavior → state adaptation → personality voices.

**When to Offer Task Creation (lines 70-73):** Three clear rules — offer to capture, confirm first, one at a time. Prevents the bot from auto-creating or overwhelming.

**Presenting Task Lists (lines 75-80):** Max 5 tasks, relevance-ordered, short format, overflow summary. ADHD-specific: avoids information dumps that trigger overwhelm.

**Handling Completions (lines 82-85):** Brief acknowledgment, no over-celebration, one natural next step max. Matches existing "celebrate without being over-the-top" voice principle.

**State-Aware Task Behavior (lines 87-96):** Each of the 6 states has a specific rule. Key highlights:
- Hyperfocus: no task mentions unless hard deadline — protects flow state
- Avoidance: references ICNU framework, offers smallest step — evidence-based approach
- Overwhelm: at most one task, no choices — reduces cognitive load
- RSD: no tasks/deadlines/incomplete work — avoids shame triggers

Total SOUL.md: 144 lines, well under the 300-line limit.
