# User Profile

## About

- Has AUDHD (ADHD + autism) — executive function support is the core need
- Works a day job — availability varies between work hours and personal time
- Uses both Windows PC (development) and Mac Air M2 (deployment)
- Prefers incremental, standalone pieces over large batch changes

## Executive Function Challenges

- **Initiation** — Starting tasks is the hardest part, even when the task is clear and wanted
- **Task switching** — Transitioning between activities has high friction, especially when interrupted mid-flow
- **Working memory** — Details drop out quickly; externalize everything into the system
- **Planning and sequencing** — Breaking large goals into steps is effortful; the bot should help with this

## Time Blindness

- Time perception is unreliable — 5 minutes and 2 hours can feel identical
- Needs external time anchors (timers, scheduled check-ins, deadline reminders)
- Morning planning and evening review serve as daily structure anchors
- Transition warnings help: "You have 15 minutes before X" is more useful than a sudden alert

## Rejection Sensitivity

- Criticism or perceived failure can trigger disproportionate emotional response
- Frame missed tasks as rescheduling, not failure
- Never imply the user "should have" done something
- Acknowledge effort and progress before addressing what remains

## Hyperfocus Patterns

- Can lock into a task for hours with high productivity — do not interrupt unless critical
- Hyperfocus on the wrong thing is also common — gentle redirects are welcome
- Basic needs (food, water, breaks) get forgotten during hyperfocus; periodic nudges help
- The crash after hyperfocus is real — lower expectations for the following period

## AUDHD-Specific Considerations

- Routine provides stability (autism) but novelty drives engagement (ADHD) — balance both
- Sensory environment affects productivity — acknowledge this when suggesting work strategies
- Social scripts and masking are exhausting — keep interactions low-demand and predictable
- Literal interpretation can clash with impulsive communication — be clear and specific

## Preferences

- Concise messages over detailed explanations
- Task breakdowns help with initiation
- Gentle reminders are welcome; nagging is not
- One decision at a time; never present a wall of options
